# Tiny Days Privacy Policy

**Effective date:** June 3, 2026

Tiny Days is a private family care tracking app for baby and household coordination. This Privacy Policy explains what information Tiny Days collects, how it is used, and how it is protected.

## Information We Collect

Tiny Days may collect and store:

- Account information, such as your sign-in identity.
- Household information, such as household membership and caregiver roles.
- Baby care information, such as feeding, sleep, diaper, notes, contractions, and care history.
- Notification information, such as device push tokens used to send household alerts.
- Location information only when you use a feature that needs it, such as a labor emergency alert.
- Microphone access only for app features you explicitly enable, such as local wake-word or voice-command experiments.

## How We Use Information

Tiny Days uses information to:

- Sync baby care records across household members.
- Show feeding, sleep, diaper, notes, contractions, and care history.
- Send household notifications, widget updates, reminders, and emergency alerts.
- Support app widgets, shortcuts, and voice-action features.
- Generate reminders, forecasts, estimates, and care predictions for your household.

## Estimates And Care Predictions

Tiny Days may use care history to show estimates, forecasts, reminders, or predictions, such as likely feeding or sleep timing. These estimates are informational only and can be incomplete, inaccurate, delayed, or wrong. They should not be treated as fact or relied on as a substitute for caregiver judgment, recommended child care practices, or advice from a licensed medical professional.

Parents and caregivers should continue to consult qualified medical professionals for child health, feeding, sleep, development, emergency, or safety concerns.

## Data Sharing

Tiny Days does not sell personal information.

Tiny Days does not use your data for advertising.

Tiny Days does not share household or baby care data with advertisers or data brokers.

Data may be processed by service providers required for the app to function, including Google Firebase services for authentication, database storage, cloud messaging, and related infrastructure.

## Data Storage

Tiny Days stores app data using Firebase services. Household data is associated with authenticated user accounts and household membership.

## Location Data

Tiny Days only uses location when a feature requires it, such as a labor emergency alert. Location is not used for advertising and is not continuously tracked by Tiny Days.

## Microphone And Voice Features

If voice or wake-word features are enabled, microphone access is used for that app feature. Tiny Days is intended to process wake-word behavior locally where supported. Tiny Days does not sell voice data or use it for advertising.

## Notifications

Tiny Days uses notifications for household alerts, reminders, emergency alerts, and widget synchronization. Push notification tokens are stored only so notifications can be sent to household devices.

## Children's Privacy

Tiny Days stores baby care information entered by parents or caregivers. The app is intended for use by parents or caregivers, not by children directly.

## Data Security

Tiny Days uses authentication and Firebase security rules to limit access to household data. Only authorized household members should be able to access shared household records.
